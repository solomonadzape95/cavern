# Cavern Studios — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

- **Players / fans** — people who follow indie games. They land from a trailer, a storefront, or word of mouth. They want to feel the studio's *taste* in ten seconds, see the games, and wishlist/buy. The site is a mood piece first, a catalog second.
- **Press & sponsors** — journalists and partners who need credibility fast: who the studio is, what they've shipped, a press kit, a way to reach out about sponsorships.
- **Prospective hires** — the "we're hiring" toast targets devs/artists/audio/marketing folks. They want to know the openings and whether this is a place with a soul.

### Brand Personality

**Hand-carved · weathered · theatrical.** A small studio that makes atmospheric games and is not afraid of the dark. Think a tavern signboard hacked from a plank, a Darkest-Dungeon parchment, a woodcut print pulled by hand — but in a mossy forest-floor palette, not the usual blood-and-soot. It takes craft seriously and marketing-speak not at all. Rough at the edges *on purpose*; precise underneath.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: dark. Mood, atmosphere, cavern. A forest-floor at dusk, not a SaaS dashboard.
- **Palette (green-grunge)** — recolor the torn template art into these, do not use the templates' brown/orange:
  - `bg` Dark moss `#3F4A3C` — dominant surface
  - `paper` Off-white `#E9E9DD` — primary text / "carved" labels
  - `olive` Muted olive `#807F51` — buttons, links, default accent
  - `sage` Sage `#A2AF9E` — subtle UI, borders, secondary text
  - `moss` Rich green `#41723B` — highlight + hover only (rare, 10%)
- **Texture is the signal**: a fine paper/grain noise sits over the whole page; key surfaces (buttons, panels, image headers) have torn/ragged edges via SVG turbulence-displacement filters and clip-paths. Edges are irregular, never a clean `rounded-xl`.
- **Anti-references** (do NOT drift toward these):
  - NOT the AI-slop look: no cyan-on-dark, no purple→blue gradients, no neon glow, no glassmorphism, no lucide-icon-above-every-heading.
  - NOT generic shadcn/Vercel: no zinc `rounded-2xl` card spam, no soft even shadows, no centered-everything hero.
  - NOT the templates' literal brown/orange dungeon palette — we keep their *texture and torn shapes*, recolored to moss/olive.
- **References**: hand-carved tavern signs, woodcut & linocut prints, torn parchment quest boards, letterpress on cheap newsprint, moss and lichen on stone, the spine-side of a shelf of old hardback books.

### Type System

- **Display**: **Germania One** (Google) — blackletter-rough display for big headings, section names, button labels, the studio name. This is the carved-signboard voice.
- **Body**: **EB Garamond** (Google) — warm old-style serif for paragraphs, captions, UI prose. Cap line length ~65–75ch; on dark bg add ~0.05–0.1 to line-height.
- **Numerals/labels** stay in EB Garamond small-caps / letter-spaced uppercase; no monospace (this is not a "developer" brand).
- **Banned reflex fonts** (do not use anywhere): Inter, Roboto, Open Sans, Geist, DM Sans/Serif, Space Grotesk, Plus Jakarta, IBM Plex, Cormorant, Cormorant Garamond, Playfair, Fraunces, Newsreader, Syne, Outfit, Instrument. Germania One + EB Garamond only.

### Layout & Density

- 4pt spacing scale with semantic tokens (`--space-xs/sm/md/lg/xl/2xl`), fluid `clamp()` on big headings (display scale ≥1.25 ratio).
- **Break the grid intentionally.** Left-anchor headings, let space sit asymmetrically. Section names are *massive* display type; copy is restrained beside/under them.
- Containers are **torn panels and hairline rules**, not soft cards. Never nest cards in cards.
- Sections scroll *over* a fixed hero. Peripheral pages open with a 75vh textured image header carrying the page name.

### Memorable Moments

1. **Hero diagonal block-reveal** — the fixed hero image assembles from a grid of tiles fading/sliding in along the corner-to-corner diagonal on load, then the massive copy rises in under a clip mask.
2. **Books on a shelf → 2.5D open** — games are spines seen side-on; clicking rotates one in perspective to present its "cover" + quick details. Tactile, like pulling a book off a shelf.
3. **Services scale-up reveal** — a shape grows from a point to fill the viewport, the section developing inside it as it opens.

### Design Principles

1. **Craft over marketing.** Copy is terse and a little mythic; no "Welcome to your dashboard!" friendliness, no feature-bragging.
2. **Texture everywhere, glow nowhere.** Depth comes from grain, torn edges, and ink-on-paper contrast — never from neon or blur.
3. **One rich green for "now".** `moss #41723B` is reserved for hover/active/highlight. Everywhere else is moss-bg + paper-ink + olive/sage.
4. **Motion is mechanical and weighty.** Reveals stagger and settle; nothing springs or bounces like a toy. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
5. **Every torn edge is intentional.** The roughness is a designed system (`GrungeButton`, `RaggedPanel`, `GrainOverlay`), not random.

## Notes for implementation

- Design tokens live in `app/globals.css` (`@theme inline`, Tailwind v4). Palette + spacing + fonts defined there.
- Fonts wired in `app/layout.tsx` via `next/font/google` → `--font-display` (Germania One), `--font-body` (EB Garamond). Remove the scaffolded Geist fonts.
- Torn edges: shared SVG `<filter>` with `feTurbulence` + `feDisplacementMap` in a root `<svg>`, referenced by `GrungeButton` / `RaggedPanel`. Grain via fixed tiling noise overlay.
- Template references for the recolor: `public/templates/button-template.png`, `public/templates/border-template.png` (keep shapes, change colors to the palette above).
